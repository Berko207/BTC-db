# Bitcoin History Timeline (2008 – June 2026)

> **Purpose**: This repository serves as a structured, factual reference database of Bitcoin's history, key events, protocol changes, market milestones, and adoption context. It is designed to provide reliable background knowledge for trading systems, on-chain analytics, DeFi integrations, research, and future Bitcoin-related projects.

## Quick Facts

- **Genesis Block**: January 3, 2009 (Block 0)
- **Creator**: Satoshi Nakamoto (pseudonymous)
- **Whitepaper**: "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" published October 31, 2008
- **Maximum Supply**: 21,000,000 BTC (hard cap)
- **Current Block Reward (June 2026)**: 3.125 BTC
- **Next Halving**: Expected ~April 2028
- **Current Status (June 2026)**: Mature digital asset class with institutional adoption, spot ETFs, and global recognition.

## Detailed Timeline

### 2008 – The Idea
- **August 18, 2008**: Domain bitcoin.org registered.
- **October 31, 2008**: Satoshi Nakamoto publishes the Bitcoin whitepaper on the cryptography mailing list.

### 2009 – Birth of Bitcoin
- **January 3, 2009**: Genesis block (Block #0) is mined. It contains the headline from *The Times*: "Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks".
- **January 12, 2009**: First Bitcoin transaction: Satoshi Nakamoto sends 10 BTC to Hal Finney.
- **October 2009**: First exchange rate established (~1 BTC = $0.00076 via New Liberty Standard).

### 2010 – First Real-World Use
- **May 22, 2010**: "Bitcoin Pizza Day" – Laszlo Hanyecz pays 10,000 BTC for two pizzas (first known commercial transaction).
- Bitcoin begins trading on exchanges.

### 2011 – Early Volatility
- Price reaches ~$1 for the first time.
- June 2011: Peak near $31 during first major bubble.
- Major exchange hacks and volatility begin shaping perception.

### 2012 – First Halving
- **November 28, 2012**: First halving – block reward drops from 50 BTC to 25 BTC.

### 2013 – Mainstream Attention
- Price surges past $1,000 in late 2013.
- Silk Road shutdown impacts sentiment.
- Mt. Gox becomes dominant exchange (later infamous collapse in 2014).

### 2014–2015 – Bear Market & Resilience
- 2014: Mt. Gox hack and bankruptcy.
- Price bottoms around $200 in early 2015.
- Development continues: Core developers improve protocol.

### 2016 – Second Halving & Recovery
- **July 9, 2016**: Second halving – reward to 12.5 BTC.
- Price recovers and begins new bull cycle.

### 2017 – Massive Bull Run
- Price reaches all-time high of approximately **$19,800** in December 2017.
- ICO boom and mainstream media coverage.
- Segregated Witness (SegWit) activated (August 2017) – major scalability upgrade.

### 2018–2019 – Crypto Winter
- Severe bear market. Price drops below $4,000.
- Focus shifts to building (Lightning Network development accelerates).

### 2020 – Third Halving & Institutional Entry
- **May 11, 2020**: Third halving – reward to 6.25 BTC.
- MicroStrategy begins corporate Bitcoin treasury strategy (Aug 2020).
- PayPal announces Bitcoin support.
- Price begins powerful bull run.

### 2021 – New All-Time Highs
- Price reaches ~**$69,000** in November 2021.
- El Salvador adopts Bitcoin as legal tender.
- Tesla briefly accepts then reverses Bitcoin payments.
- Taproot upgrade activated (November 2021) – improved privacy and smart contract capabilities.

### 2022 – Bear Market & FTX Collapse
- Prolonged bear market.
- **November 2022**: FTX collapse sends shockwaves; Bitcoin bottoms around **$15,500**.
- Focus on self-custody and "Not your keys, not your coins" narrative strengthens.

### 2023 – Recovery & ETF Anticipation
- Price recovers above $40,000 by end of year.
- Spot Bitcoin ETF applications gain momentum in the US.

### 2024 – ETFs, Halving & New ATH
- **January 2024**: U.S. SEC approves spot Bitcoin ETFs (launched mid-January).
- **March 13, 2024**: Bitcoin reaches new all-time high above **$73,000**.
- **April 20, 2024**: Fourth halving – block reward drops to **3.125 BTC**.
- Strong institutional inflows via ETFs.

### 2025 – New Cycle Highs
- Bitcoin achieves a new all-time high of approximately **$126,000** in October 2025.
- Increased corporate and nation-state interest (strategic reserve discussions).
- Continued maturation of the asset class.

### 2026 (as of June 16, 2026)
- Bitcoin trading in the **$60,000 – $98,000** range during the first half of the year.
- Post-2025 peak consolidation phase.
- Growing integration into traditional finance (ETFs, futures, custody solutions).
- Protocol remains stable with ongoing layer-2 development (Lightning Network, etc.).
- Next halving expected in 2028.

| Taproot       | November 2021    | Better privacy, efficiency, and smart contract support   |
| ...           | Ongoing          | Continued soft forks and improvements                    |

## Major Market Cycles (Approximate)

Bitcoin has historically followed ~4-year cycles tied to halvings:

1. **2011–2013** cycle
2. **2013–2017** cycle  
3. **2017–2021** cycle
4. **2021–2025** cycle (new ATH in late 2025)
